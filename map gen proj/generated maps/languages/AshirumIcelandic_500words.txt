ASHIRUM ICELANDIC - 500 Common Word Translations
Fantasy Language based on AshirumIcelandic name patterns
Linguistic roots: Old Norse (8th-13th c.) + Modern Icelandic blend
Attested Old Norse and Icelandic words used where possible; constructed fill
follows Norse phonotactics.

KEY SOURCE WORDS:
  Old Norse: maðr (man), kona (woman), barn (child), land (land), vatn (water),
  eldr (fire), himinn (sky), jörð (earth), dagr (day), nótt (night), veðr (weather),
  vegr (way/road), fara (to go), vera (to be), gefa (to give), taka (to take),
  sjá (to see), segja (to say), vita (to know), vilja (to want), ganga (to walk),
  koma (to come), standa (to stand), hafa (to have), gera (to do/make),
  mikill (great/large), góðr (good), gamall (old), ungr (young), sterkr (strong)

  Modern Icelandic: já (yes), nei (no), og (and), en (but), af (of/from),
  með (with), við (at/by), til (to/until), um (about), frá (from),
  fara (to go), vera (to be), gera (to do), sjá (to see), tala (to talk),
  stór (large), lítill (small), góður (good), fallegur (beautiful), sterkur (strong)

NOTE: Ashirum blends the harder consonants of Old Norse (ð, þ, kv, sk, str, gr)
with the melodic vowel shifts of Modern Icelandic (au, ei, ó, æ, ö).
Name list patterns: Eld-Borg, Eld-Fell, Gjall-Borg, Krafla-Dal, Raud-Fell,
Skall-Borg, Sur-Fell, Surtsey, Hekla, Grimr, Skarphedin — favour hard
consonant-initial stems with -r endings and compound structures.

Old Norse sounds preserved in Ashirum:
  ð   = voiced dental fricative (as in English "the")
  þ   = voiceless dental fricative (as in English "thin")

  æ   = front open vowel (as in "cat" but longer)
  ý   = long front rounded vowel
  á   = long open back vowel (as in "father")
  ó   = long back rounded vowel
  ú   = long high back rounded vowel

  sk-  = initial cluster (Skarphedin, Skall-Borg)

  gr-  = voiced stop + liquid (grjót, gras)
  kv-  = labio-velar (kvenna)
  -rð  = final cluster (jörð, veðr)
