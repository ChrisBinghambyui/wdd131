SYLVARIRUM NAVAJO - 500 Common Word Translations
Fantasy Language based on SylvarirumNavajo name patterns
Linguistic roots: Diné bizaad (Navajo) — real roots with constructed fill
Attested Navajo words used where possible; constructed Navajo-pattern words fill gaps.

KEY SOURCE WORDS (Diné bizaad / Navajo):
  nizhóní (beautiful/good), hózhó (harmony/beauty/balance), shí (I/my),
  nił (with you), diné (people/person), dinétah (homeland), nahasdzáán (earth),
  yádiłhił (sky), jį́ (day), tł'ęę' (night), tó (water), kǫ' (fire),
  tsé (rock/stone), dzil (mountain), tsin (wood/tree), niyol (wind),
  chizh (firewood), ałhą́ą́' (together), haa (how/what), haash (what is it),
  shijéí (my heart), bił (with it), naaki (two), tááʼ (three), dįʼ (four),
  ashdlaʼ (five), hashtł'ish (mud), dził (mountain — variant)

NOTE: Navajo is a tonal, polysynthetic language. Tones (high/low) are marked
with accent: á (high), a (low). Nasalization marked with ą. Glottal stop = ʼ.
In Sylvarirum, tones are softened for fantasy use but phonemic shapes preserved.
Constructed words follow Navajo phonotactics: CV, CVC, CCVC patterns with
characteristic consonants: zh, sh, ts, tł, dz, gh, hw, kw, łʼ.

PHONETIC GUIDE FOR SYLVARIRUM NAVAJO
===================================================================================

Navajo sounds preserved in Sylvarirum:
  ł   = voiceless lateral fricative (like Welsh "ll" in Llanberis)
  tł  = lateral affricate (t + ł combined)
  ts  = affricate (as in "cats")
  zh  = voiced fricative (as in "measure")
  ʼ   = glottal stop (brief throat closure)
  ą   = nasalized a (air through nose)
  į   = nasalized i
  High tone: á, í, ó, ú (marked with acute accent)
  Low tone: a, i, o, u (unmarked)

Syllable structure:
  Navajo favors: CV, CVC, CCVC
  Common initial clusters: ts-, tł-, sh-, zh-, hw-, dz-, gh-
  Common endings: -ʼ (glottal), -ł (lateral), -d, -zh, -s

Word-building patterns from name list:
  bi-    = his/her/its (possessive prefix — very common)
  shi-   = my (1st person prefix)
  ni-    = your (2nd person prefix)
  nihi-  = our/their (plural prefix)
  -go    = subordinate clause marker / manner suffix
  -di    = locative (at/in a place)
  -gi    = locative variant
  -íí    = nominalizer (the one that...)
  -ígíí  = relative nominalizer (that which...)
  naagháh = going around / traveling (movement root)
  hózhó  = beauty/harmony/balance (core cultural concept)
