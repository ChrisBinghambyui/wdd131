NORDAL RUSSIAN - 500 Common Word Translations
Fantasy Language based on NordalRussian name patterns
Linguistic roots: Old Church Slavonic (primary base) + Medieval Russian,
layered with Polish (heaviest), Czech/Bohemian, Lithuanian/Baltic,
Hungarian vowel harmony, Wallachian/Moldavian, Rus Principalities, Serbian/Bulgarian.

KEY SOURCE WORDS:
  OCS: językŭ (language), zemlja (earth), voda (water), ogňŭ (fire), nebо (sky),
  světŭ (light/world), slovo (word), imę (name), věra (faith/truth), vladŭ (rule/power),
  bratŭ (brother), mati (mother), sŭnŭ (son), ljudije (people), knędzĭ (prince/lord),
  věšti (thing), choditi (to go), věděti (to know), dati (to give), byti (to be),
  Polish: pan (lord/sir), ziemia (earth), woda (water), ogień (fire), niebo (sky),
  Czech: zem (earth), voda, oheň, nebe, slovo — mirroring OCS closely,
  Lithuanian: žemė (earth), vanduo (water), ugnis (fire), dangus (sky),
  Hungarian: föld (earth), víz (water), tűz (fire), ég (sky), ember (person),
  vár (fortress/castle), úr (lord), király (king)

NOTE: Nordal blends OCS's formal ecclesiastical register with the hard consonant
stacks of Polish (sz, cz, rz, szcz), Czech softness (ř, č, ž), Lithuanian flow
(-as, -is endings), and Hungarian vowel harmony (-vár, -ság, -ár suffixes).
The result is a language that feels weighty, dynastic, and fortress-edged.

PHONETIC GUIDE FOR NORDAL RUSSIAN
===================================================================================

Old Church Slavonic sounds:
  ŭ   = ultra-short back vowel (reduced, often silent in modern descendants)
  ĭ   = ultra-short front vowel (reduced)
  ę   = nasal front vowel (as in French "vin" — Polish preserved this as ę)
  ą   = nasal back vowel (as in French "on" — Polish preserves as ą)
  ě   = front vowel between e and ie (yat — disappeared in most modern Slavic)
  š   = voiceless palatal fricative (sh)
  ž   = voiced palatal fricative (zh)
  č   = palatal affricate (ch)
  št  = Bulgarian/OCS cluster (šč in Russian)
  žd  = Bulgarian/OCS cluster (zd in Russian)

Polish consonant stacks preserved in Nordal:
  sz  = š (heavy sh)
  cz  = č (heavy ch)
  rz  = ž (heavy zh, once a trill)
  szcz = šč (the infamous Polish cluster)
  dź  = soft dz
  ń   = soft n

Lithuanian softness:
  -as / -is endings on nouns (Gediminas, Vytautas, Kernavė)
  flow of liquid consonants -l-, -r- within words

Hungarian vowel harmony flavoring:
  -vár suffix = fortress/castle (Visegrád, Esztergom)
  -ság suffix = abstract noun quality
  Front vowels group: e, é, i, ö, ő, ü, ű
  Back vowels group: a, á, o, ó, u, ú

Word-building patterns:
  OCS prě-  = over/trans- (prěchodŭ, prěstorŭ)
  OCS sŭ-   = together/with- (sŭtvoriti, sŭrdĭce)
  OCS vŭ-   = in/into- (vŭstokŭ, vzęti)
  OCS po-   = after/along- (poslidĭnŭ, pokoi)
  OCS na-   = on/upon/at- (načęti, namŭ)
  OCS ot-   = from/away- (otiti, opisati)
  -ĭstvo    = abstract noun (хudožĭstvo, dělanĭje)
  -ĭnŭ      = adjectival suffix (věrĭnŭ, krasnŭ)
