VERDATHI ABORIGINAL - 500 Common Word Translations
Fantasy Language based on VerdathiAboriginal name patterns
(Phonetic patterns derived from: Adjungbilly, Bunjil, Cooinda, Cooee, Gundagai,
 Gurulmundi, Iluka, Indooroopilly, Uluru, Wollongong, Yuin, Boorabin, Kurrajong, etc.)

NOTE: Where no direct Aboriginal equivalent exists, words are constructed using
attested phonemic patterns: open vowels (a, i, u, oo), consonant clusters (ng, lj, mb,
nd, rr, wl), and characteristic suffixes (-indi, -ulla, -amba, -oora, -inga, -urru).

PHONETIC GUIDE FOR VERDATHI ABORIGINAL
===================================================================================

Vowels:
  a   = open back vowel (as in "father")
  i   = front vowel (as in "see")
  u   = back rounded (as in "moon")
  oo  = long back vowel (as in "cool")
  ai  = diphthong (as in "sky")

Consonant clusters characteristic of this language:
  ng  = nasal velar (as in "sing") — very common word-initially
  dj  = palatal affricate (as in "judge")
  rr  = retroflex or trilled r
  mb  = prenasalized bilabial stop
  nd  = prenasalized dental stop
  lj  = lateral palatal

Suffix patterns:
  -amba / -imba  = verb/action suffix
  -indi / -inga  = locative or participial
  -ulla / -ulla  = possessive/relational
  -oo / -oong    = nominal or directional
  -urru / -urra  = animate being marker
  -bali          = diminutive / young
